In the United States hundreds of thousands of people celebrated Easter by snapping up the new iPad tablet computer. And news organisations worldwide are desperately hoping that it can do for the news media what the iPod did for the music industry - provide a simple, convenient way for people to pay for highly-packaged electronic news.
 
Tonight, a special program. Once again, we're wrestling with the future viability of quality journalism, print journalism in particular. Does it lie in paywalls on the web, and tablets in the hand?

* Marc Frons, Chief Technology Officer for Digital Operations at The New York Times
* France, media commentator Frederic Filloux, author of the influential blog Monday Note. 
* Rupert Murdoch
