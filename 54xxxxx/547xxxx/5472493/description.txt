Essential early 80's electro... Man Parrish drew his inspiration from artists such as Brian Eno and Kraftwerk as well as the burgeoning hip-hop scene in New York City (particularly the circle around Afrika Bambaataa). His instrumentals such as "Hip Hop Be Bop (Don't Stop)" have been highly influential in the establishment of producer-oriented electronic dance music. He was also a central figure in the downtown NYC art scene of the time. In his own words: "I hung around with the Andy Warhol crowd. We grew up in the glitter, freakazoid 70's free love and drugs and that kind of stuff. So my manifestation of it in the 80's was just an extension of what I already knew. It was performance and theatre art and music combined into one." Here is the master's 1983 debut, a non-stop robot-groove affair named after the groundbreaking "Hip Hop Be Bop," which is presented in two parts. Highly recommended for fans of Kraftwerk and vintage electronics.
