SYNOPSIS

Two hazardous expeditions, one in summer and one in winter, were undertaken to 
shoot this film in Ladakh and Zanskar. In the legendary Buddhist monastery of 
Lingshed in Zanskar, at a height of 4000 metres, the team successfully managed 
to film the creation of a sand mandala from beginning to end. Being able to 
produce such a unique & sacred Buddhist object demands many years of induction 
on the part of the monks involved. This film on the artistic and cultural history 
of the Ladakh and Zanskar districts of the Indian State of Jammu-Kashmir 
demonstrates the ascetic but fulfilled life of monks and peasants in a remote 
area of the Tran Himalayas. At the same time the film attempts to provide a 
sensitive introduction to the intricacies of Tibetan Buddhism.
