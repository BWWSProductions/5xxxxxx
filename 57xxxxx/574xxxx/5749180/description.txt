We are the cultural descendants of the Reformation era, says Professor Brad S. Gregory in these lectures on one of the most tumultuous and consequential periods in all of European history.

Regardless of whether we ourselves are religious, says Professor Gregory, our modern preference for belief bolstered by doctrine is "a long-term legacy of the efforts to educate, to catechize, to indoctrinate, that began in a widespread way during the 16th century."
