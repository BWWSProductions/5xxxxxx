Additional notes:  A Menhir is a monolithic stone; they can stand alone or in 
=================  circular  groups, as seen at Stonehenge. No one is quite sure
                   what their purpose is, but many have been found all around 
                   the world. Modern historians hypothesize that they act as 
                   boundary markers, enormous grave stones, calendars or even 
                   as the sites of Druidic sacrifices. The word itself is an 
                   amalgamation of two Breton words: men meaning stone, and 
                   hir meaning long.
