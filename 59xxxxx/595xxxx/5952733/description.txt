SYNOPSIS

A co-production of Tibet House U.S. in New York and Link TV, the program brings 
together for the first time ever one of the world's most important spiritual 
leaders with renowned journalists, economists, environmentalists, and politicians 
to discuss the ethical dilemmas of the new millennium. With allegations of 
government subterfuge, corporate malfeasance scandals and a devastating global 
warming crisis dominating the headlines, this unprecedented television program 
gives a rare glimpse into the enlightening ethical teachings, peaceful nature, 
and often surprising humour of the Dalai Lama. The program shows highlights from 
four panels: Ethics and the Environment (with David Crow, Randall Hayes, Paul Hawken), Ethics and the Media (with Amy Goodman, Katrina vanden Heuvel, Susan Sarandon); Ethics and Economics (with Ben Cohen, Amy Domini, Russell Simmons); and Ethics and the Politics of Peace (with Dr. Helen Caldicott, Dennis Kucinich, and 
Rev. Al Sharpton). The discussions are intercut with interviews from the panelists, who provide personal insights into their impressions of the dialogue and what ethics means to them. The program was filmed in September, 2003 at New York's Town Hall.
