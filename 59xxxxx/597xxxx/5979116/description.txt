For english-only speaking:
This book is in german language. English title would be: What was the US Army at Nazi national redoubt (Alpine Fortress) really looking for (2006). Markus Schmitzberger's groundbreaking book on the unbelievable mysteries surrounding the Nazi national redoubt known as Alpine Fortress which would serve as the last retreat of Nazi elite as Germany would slowly be overrun by allied forces. At least that's how it was presented to the world by the allied intelligence services who expected to meet there large storage spaces and defense buildings together with fanatical soldiers willing to defend it till their death by using the newest weaponry available to them. But could it be that the last retreat never was there at all but was instead somewhere else, in an area in Austria known as Quarz? It is there that the last Nazi units and weaponry were delivered to. Quarz is an underground area with a mysterious past. Author's research shows that it was here that the last desperate attempt to construct new superweapons found its place. Why else would the area be so incredibly defended? And there is more. As he shows it was at Quarz that the production of heavy water took place that was only needed to supply an upcomming atom bomb. Was this the big secret that the allied forces were so eager to get a hold of? It seems it must of been even more important than the project for an intercontinental ballistic rocket known as Amerika-Rocket. Also the presence of the powerful SS general Hans Kammler speak volumes that something occured there that must stay suppressed to this very day. The author also uncovers some completely unknown concentration camps in the area, why aren't these documented at all? 235 pages.
