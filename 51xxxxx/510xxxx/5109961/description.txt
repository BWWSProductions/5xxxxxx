John Mortimer Presents - the Trials of Marshall Hall  - BBC Radio Drama Series


Sir Edward Marshall-Hall, KC, (Brighton, September 16, 1858 - February 24, 1927) was an English barrister who had a formidable reputation as an orator. He successfully defended many people accused of notorious murders and became known as "The Great Defender".

True stories of crime and trial in a bygone age introduced by John Mortimer and starring Tom Baker.

Five episodes of approx 30 minutes each.

First broadcast from November to December 1996.

Produced  By John Taylor and Michael Fox



Episode 1 - The Black Trunk:
Tom Baker is Britain's celebrated criminal barrister, Sir Edward Marshall Hall, in his first case, fought in 1894.

Episode 2 - The Camden Town Murder:
Did Bob Wood slit the throat of Emily Dimmock? Sir Edward Marshall Hall sets out to prove Bob's innocence.

Episode 3 - A Hint Of Arsenic:
The barrister is determined to prove Harold Greenwood innocent of killing his first wife.

Episode 4 - A Death At Christmas:
Ex-soldier Frederick Rothwell Holt is charged with murdering his lover. But is he responsible for his actions?

Episode 5 - The Case Of The Green Bicycle:
Did Ronald Light shoot a woman cyclist dead? The feted barrister sets out to prove his innocence.
