The Beatles on Record
Aired:BBC Two on Saturday 5 September 2009

A new documentary featuring never-heard-before fragments of conversation, along with interviews and rare footage of The Beatles in the studio. The joint season, celebrating the Fab Four's enduring legacy and continuing influence. The Beatles On Record offers a concise history of The Beatles in the studio and sits alongside other highlights including the first TV showing of The Beatles: The First U.S. Visit, the Maysles brothers' film charting the Fab Four's arrival in America in 1964.

In The Beatles on Record the focus is on the process behind making the art that is the Beatles music. The documentary takes us from Please Please Me to the recording of the historic Abbey Road LP with in studio footage, pictures and audio. In keeping with the theme of creating art, we get a behind the scenes look at how the band matured as artists, songwriters and musicians. Dipping into the massive Beatles archive of outtakes and audio.

John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, Ringo Starr and producer George Martin narrate a charting of the Beatles' evolution as musicians and songwriters, with recordings made at Abbey Road Studios beginning in 1962.
