This is the most complete collection of stuff for Apple II computers.

Apple II was the first popular home computer begun producing in 1977. As Apple IIe modification it was produced until 1993. In 1986 16-bit home computer Apple IIgs was produced, it had hardware compatibility with Apple II. Many Apple II 8-bit computers were in educational institutions of USA. About 5 million copmuters were sold (comparable to MSX and Atari ST). About 1000 games were produced for Apple II and about 250 for Apple IIgs.

= Specifications =

 Apple II+ (1979)
CPU MOS Technology 6502 1 MHz; RAM 48 Kb (64 Kb with language card); video: 280 x 192 x 6 colors (palette 6 colors), text: 40 x 24, no low-case characters; sound: 1-bit; BASIC in ROM; tape or floppy 5.25" 140 Kb.

 Apple IIe (1983)
Differences from Apple II+: RAM 64 Kb (in common expanded to 128 Kb); video: low-case characters appeared. Newer revisions supported 80-column text expansion card wich gave additional 64 Kb of RAM and new video modes 560 x 192 b/w, 140 x 192 x 16 colors. Many were expanded with a sound chip.

 Apple IIgs (1986)
CPU: Western Design Center 65C816 2.8 MHz (can work at 1 MHz); RAM 256 Kb (in common expanded to 1-2 Mb); video: 320 x 200 x 16 colors (palette 3200 colors), text 80 x 24; sound: 8-bit, 15 voices, stereo (mono without an expansion card), 64 Kb wavetable RAM; 3.5" 800 Kb or 5.25" 140 Kb floppy; hardware compatibility with Apple II.
