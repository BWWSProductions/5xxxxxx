Later, With Jools Holland.s36eo5.(14th May 2010)[DTV-RIP-Divx]

First Shown On BBC 2 On Friday 14th May 2010


Description:

Joining Jools are James Murphy's LCD Soundsystem performing tracks from their third - and possibly final - album This Is Happening; from Melbourne, Australia, Crowded House, led by original member Neil Finn, with tracks from their new album Intriguer; Kelis, who first stormed the charts in 2000, performing tracks from her new Fleshtone album; and Brooklyn based five-piece The National, with music from their new High Violet long player. Plus Tracey Thorn, half of British duo Everything But The Girl, performs a number from her album Love and Its Opposite.


Crowded House

Crowded House release their sixth album Intriguer in June, and give us a taste of it on this week's show. It's the Australian rockers' fourth appearance on Later...

LCD Soundsystem

LCD Soundsystem return for the second time. They will be playing songs from their forthcoming third album This Is Happening, which James Murphy says will be their last.

Kelis

Kelis is back after a four year break (during which time she training as a cordon bleu chef!) with a new album Flesh Tone. The record sees her add a new dance angle to her repertoire.

The National

The National come to the studio for a second time to bring new tracks from their fifth album, High Violet, recorded in their own converted garage studio.

Tracey Thorn

Tracey Thorn's second solo album Love and its Opposite is released this month on Strange Feeling Records. She returns to the Later studio to play a track at the piano.

Pete Molinari

Pete Molinari, a country blues singer from Chatham in Kent, makes his debut on Later. His third album is titled A Train Bound For Glory and is released in June.

Sam Taylor-Wood

Sam Taylor-Wood comes to chat to us this week about the release of her film 'Nowhere Boy', which depicts the early years of The Beatle John Lennon's life.
