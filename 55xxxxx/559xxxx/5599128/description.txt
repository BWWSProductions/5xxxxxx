  Reasonable Doubt is the debut album of American rapper Jay-Z, released June 
25, 1996. The album features production by DJ Premier, Ski, Knobody and Clark 
Kent, and guest appearances by Memphis Bleek, Mary J. Blige,and The Notorious
B.I.G., among others. The album peaked at number 23 on the Billboard 200, 
received platinum status in 2002, and sold 1.5 million copies as of 2006. 
Reasonable Doubt has received strong critical reviews and has been heralded 
as Jay-Z's "crowning achievement", "a seminal work" and an "undisputed 
classic".
