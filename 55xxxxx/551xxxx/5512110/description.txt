First Shown On BBC 2 On Friday 23rd April 2010




Description:

Episode 2 of Later Live featured Seattle-based rockers Band of Horses visit the studio to play tracks from their new album Infinite Arms, and indie pop star Kate Nash performs songs from My Best Friend Is You, the follow-up to her 2007 release Made of Bricks. Also appearing are ska revivalist Jerry Dammers and his Spatial AKA Orchestra, Pennsylvanian jazz singer Melody Gardot and former Cream star Jack Bruce. Plus, rapper Plan B makes his debut appearance on the show with numbers from his second album The Defamation of Strickland Banks. An extended edition can be seen on Friday at 11.50pm

Description:


Band Of Horses

The group leave their South Carolina base to play in the Later studio. They're appearing at Reading & Leeds this summer and release their third album, Infinite Arms, in May.

Kate Nash

After a break from the limelight Kate Nash returns with the follow up to her debut number 1 album. Her new album, My Best Friend Is You, is produced by Bernard Butler.

Jerry Dammer's Spatial AKA Orchestra

The orchestra reinterprets the work of Sun Ra, complete with massive spaceship and 21 musicians including Rico Rodriguez and Zoe Rahman.

Plan B

With his new album 'The Defamation of Strickland Banks' Plan B joins us for the second time in the studio, with a new character and a sharp suit to match.

Melody Gardot

The 25 year old singer songwriter Melody Gardot has gained acclaim over the past year topping jazz charts and being compared to the likes of Norah Jones and Diana Krall.

Jack Bruce

The Cream vocalist and bassist has recently released a biography, Jack Bruce Composing Himself, and joins Jools for a chat in the studio.
