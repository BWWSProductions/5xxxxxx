The film about 1930s and '40s, a masterpiece. Bogdanovich convincingly recreates the feel and look of the 1930s with his nostalgic look at life on the open road. The film was nominated for four Academy Awards, including Best Screenplay; 11-year-old O'Neal was nominated for Best Supporting Actress.

Theatrical Release: May 9, 1973.

Filmed in Florida, Kansas, Missouri, and California. 
Actress Tatum O'Neal's film debut, also won Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress. She turned 11 while shooting the film. Director Peter Bogdanovich shot the film in black-and-white because he felt the actors looked too healthy in color. The film's music includes old recordings from the collection of Warner Bros.
