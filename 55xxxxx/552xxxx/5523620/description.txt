Description: 

Joining Jools for the third show of this series are Damon Albarn and Jamie Hewlett's multimillion-selling creation Gorillaz, who are making their live debut on the show playing tunes from their latest acclaimed album Plastic Beach, with a host of special guests.

Laura Marling, who at 20 has already seen herself compared to Bob Dylan and Joni Mitchell, is playing songs off her remarkable new album I Speak Because I Can. From Athens, Georgia, comes the band Drive-By Truckers, who have southern soul quite literally in their blood. The six-piece are performing a tune or two from their new album The Big To Do.

The multi-talented Mos Def is also in the studio, to play a number from his incredible The Ecstatic album as well as possibly joining in with one of the other acts. Making her UK TV debut is Michigan-born Diane Birch, who has been causing a stir in the US with her album Bible Belt over the last few months. And jazz legend Bobby McFerrin performs a number from his new VOCAbuLarieS album.

Laura Marling

Laura Marling Returns to the Later studio, having made her TV debut on the show in 2007. She has just scored her first top ten hit with second album I Speak Because I Can.

Gorillaz 

Gorillaz finally make their physical TV debut on Later, with a whole host of guests, including Bobby Womack, Gruff Rhys, Kano, De La Soul and Little Dragon.

Mos Def

Musician, Rapper and actor Mos Def is a man of many talents. He joins us in the Later studio not only to perform a track from 2009 album The Ecstatic but also to join Gorillaz on a number too.

Drive-By Truckers

Athens, Georgia-based Drive-By Truckers wrote 25 songs in 25 days during intense recording sessions for new album, The Big To Do. They'll be visiting the Later studio for the first time this series.

Diane Birch

Diane Birch is a 27-year-old singer songwriter who has already drawn comparisons to the greats such as Carole King. Her debut album Bible Belt is out this week as she makes her TV debut in the Later studio.

Bobby McFerrin

Bobby McFerrin's trademark style often sounds like two or three singers at once. Most famous for his 1988 hit Don't Worry Be Happy, the ten-time Grammy winner released latest album VOCAbuLarieS this year.
