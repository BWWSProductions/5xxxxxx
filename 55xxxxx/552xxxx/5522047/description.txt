An indispensable collection of early films (not all are technically "firsts") from some of the most celebrated directors in cinematic history. Highlights include Charlotte et son Jules (1958, 13 mins.), Jean-Luc Godard's amusing and entertaining short about a man and a woman who jab at each other in mocking and playful fits and starts. Also included is the rarely-screened 24 Heures de la vie d'un Clown (1946, 18 mins.), Jean-Pierre Melville's first foray into filmmaking, and Alain Resnais' brilliant industrial film Le Chant du Styrene (1957, 14 mins.). L'Amour Existe (Maurice Pialat, 1961, 19 mins.), Le Coup du Berger (Jacques Rivette, 1957, 27 mins.), Historie D'eau (Francois Truffaut & Jean-Luc Godard, 1958, 12 mins.), Le Laboratorie de L'angoisse (Patrice Laconte, 1971, 11 mins.), and Les Surmenes (Jacques Doniol-Valcroze, 1957, 19 mins) round out this remarkable set.
