Trio sonatas for two oboes, bassoon and b.c.   In places this is comparable to the best of instrumental Bach - who, with Telemann, heaped praise upon him - though stylistically more intense (this is two CDs of solid counterpoint and chromatic development). And yet Zelenka is hardly known, neither for his instrumental works such as these, nor his (some would argue) even more accomplished sacred choral works. 

Further, this music is full of eccentric surprises (eg. the weirdness-from-nowhere at around 1:30 of the third mvt of Sonata 05).  In short, marvellous music of the late Baroque.

I own, or am familiar with, several recordings of these trio sonatas, and this particular version is, in my opinion, the best of those in terms of overall tone and the unrelenting interplay of the voices.  Lively and well-balanced.
