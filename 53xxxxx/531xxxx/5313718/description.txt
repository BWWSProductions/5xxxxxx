Episode 2: "Wedding Crashers"

"The Buried Life: What Do You Want To Do Before You Die?" tells the story of four friends who make a list of 100 things to do before you die and their journey across North America to accomplish it. The film was shot throughout Canada and United States including: Nevada, California, Oregon, Idaho, British Columbia and Washington. The idea for the name originated from an 1852 Matthew Arnold poem entitled "The Buried Life".

On April 18, 2009 an article in The New York Times announced that a serial television version of their documentary entited "The Buried Life" was chosen by MTV for development. The show has been selected as a flagship reality program that will usher in MTV's shift away from the superficial content currently dominating the network's programming towards the production of more socially conscious media. The network has ordered eight weekly half-hour episodes.
