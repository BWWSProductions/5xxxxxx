Grey Gardens (1975) is a documentary depicting the everyday
lives of the two Edith Beales, a reclusive socialite mother
and daughter of the same name who lived at Grey Gardens, a
decrepit mansion in the wealthy Georgica Pond neighborhood
of East Hampton, New York. The two women were the aunt and
first cousin of Jacqueline Bouvier Kennedy Onassis and
lived together at Grey Gardens for decades with limited
funds, resulting in squalor and almost total isolation.
