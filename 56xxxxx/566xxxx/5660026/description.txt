It's hard to know which spread faster - the flames of the 
Great Fire of London or the prejudice-fuelled suspicion as
to who started it. So says the latest instalment of the 
history-revisited strand Bloody Foreigners that explains 
why the familiar tale of the accident in 1666 may not be 
the whole story. Computer effects chart an aerial route of 
the conflagration's progress (picture a smoke-damaged 
Google Street View), while historians discuss how Dutch 
and then French residents in the capital came in for 
violent retribution. Some of the reconstructions look a bit 
unconvincing - one baker under attack resembles Bradley 
from EastEnders in a blond wig - but it's interesting to see 
how the blame game continued to smoulder long after the 
inferno had been extinguished.
