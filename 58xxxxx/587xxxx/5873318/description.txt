[u]The Debate: Pakistan's Political Crises[/u] 

Losing control: what the creeping radicalization of Pakistan means for that country, its neighbours and global security.

[u]Guests:[/u]

[b]Lisa Curtis[/b] is a senior research fellow at The Heritage Foundation, focusing on analyzing America's economic, security, and political relationships with India, Pakistan, Afghanistan, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh and Nepal.

[b]Tarek Fatah[/b] is author of Chasing a Mirage: The Tragic Illusion of an Islamic State. His next book The Jew is Not My Enemy is being published by McClelland & Stewart in October . For more information see TarekFatah.com.

[b]Murtaza Haider[/b] is a professor in the Ted Rogers School of Management at Ryerson University. Murtaza reported for The Frontier Post and The News in Islamabad during 1992-94, and was vice president of the Pakistan Federal Union of Journalist's Islamabad Chapter in 1993.

[b]Eric Margolis[/b] is a frequent media commentator on international affairs. He is a regular columnist for [i]Lew Rockwell[/i] and [i]Huffington Post[/i] as well as papers in the Gulf and Malaysia. He is the author, most recently, of [i]American Raj: Liberation or Domination[/i]. Visit [url=http://www.ericmargolis.com/]ericmargolis.com[/url].
