Is Keynesism the answer to our economic woes?

1) [u]The Debate: Rethinking Keynes[/u] 

From the pundits to the president: everyone, it seems, is crazy for Keynes. But is Keynesism the answer to our economic woes?

[u]Guests:[/u]

[b]Brad DeLong[/b] is a professor in the Department of Economics at U.C. Berkeley, and chair of the Berkeley International and Area Studies. He writes a blog called: [url=http://delong.typepad.com/]Grasping Reality with Both Hands[/url].

[b]Don Drummond[/b] is a senior VP and chief economist with TD Bank Financial Group.

[b]Tim Kane[/b] is a senior fellow in research and policy at the Kauffman Foundation, and was the lead editor and author of the 2007 Index of Economic Freedom. He is also a contributor to the blog: Growthology.

[b]Jeff Madrick[/b] is editor of Challenge magazine, visiting professor at The Cooper Union, and director of policy research at the Schwartz Center for Economic Policy Analysis. He is author of The Case for Big Government.
