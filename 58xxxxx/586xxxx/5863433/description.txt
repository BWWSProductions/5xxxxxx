More Guns, Less Crime

More Guns, Less Crime is a book by John Lott that examines how violent crime rates change when states pass "shall issue" concealed carry laws. He presents the results of his statistical analysis of crime data for every county in the United States  during 18 years from 1977 to 1994. The book expands on an earlier study published in 1997 by Lott and his co-author David Mustard in The Journal of Legal Studies.[1] Lott also examines the effects of gun control laws, including the Brady Law.
