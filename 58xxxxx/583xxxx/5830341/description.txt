The Alexandria Quartet is a tetralogy of novels by British writer Lawrence Durrell, published between 1957 and 1960.  the books present four perspectives on a single set of events and characters in Alexandria, Egypt, before and during World War II.

The Quartet offers the same sequence of events to us through several points of view, allowing individual perspectives to change over time about a set of events and characters in Alexandria, Egypt, before and during World War II.

The four novels: Justine (1957); Balthazar (1958); Mountolive (1958); Clea (1960).
