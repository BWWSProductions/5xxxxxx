This volume is one of a series of new volumes to emerge from the project on Sex,Marriage,and Family and the Religions of the Book, undertaken by the Center for the Study of Law and Religion at Emory University.The project seeks to take stock of the dramatic transformation of marriage and familyl ife in the world today and to craft enduring solutions to the many new problems that transformation has occasioned.The project is interdisciplinary in methodology: It seeks to bring the ancient wisdom of religious traditions and the modern sciences of law,health,public policy,the social sciences,and the humanities in to greater conversation and common purpose.
