Therion Goes Classic - At the Palace Hall/Bucharest/Romania, on December 9th, Therion plays with full symphonic orchestra, choir and international opera stars. The band will go on stage with nearly 70 musicians of the Academic Radio Choir, 100 instrumentists of the Romanian Radio Orchestra and guest classical singers from Vienna, awarded as best opera-voices world-wide. Markus Stollenwerk, who joined the famous German band Rage for their "Lingua Mortis" album will be the conductor of this world-premiere event. The repertoire includes famous classical pieces in Therion's version, as well as Therion songs with symphonic orchestra, some of them never played live before.

The project is done with kind support of the Swedish Embassy in Romania.

"Therion Goes Classic" concert is part of a project called "Ars musicae, ars medicinae - music against cancer" developed by Aquarius Foundation/Romania; all thanks for the initiative go to Therion. Funds raised following the concert are aimed to the treatment of children with cancer in Romania in 2007. Organisers also thank Wolfgang Milchrahm/Austria for his support.

For fans from outside Romania, accommodation has been booked for Eur 10 and Eur 45 in several hostels & hotels in Bucharest. Just let us know you want to share one of the nice hostels or hotels with other Therion fans, and we will provide you details on request.

Therion band members are going to be met on Friday 8th, in a fan-meeting session of a special kind.

The meeting will start with a half an hour lecture of Special Guest Uli Jon Roth, where he presents his experience with classical and rock music and his ideas on the link between music and medicine. This is followed by open discussions with the audience where both Uli and the band will answer questions, and by a press conference of 15 minutes.

A signing session of 45 minutes will end up the meeting.

Venue: the conference hall, Students' House of Culture "Grigore Preoteasa," Calea Plevnei 61. Date 8.12.2006, 17:00 hours.
Admission: 2 EUR
