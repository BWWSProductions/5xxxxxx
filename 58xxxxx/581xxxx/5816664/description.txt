Momotaro - Umi no Shinpei, the classic animated japanese propaganda movie from WWII. It was released in 1945, only a few weeks before the Japanese capitulation. The film was thought to have been lost during the American occupation, until one last surviving copy resurfaced in 1984.
The film is noted for it's high quality of production and many musical scenes. At 74 minutes, it was the first full lenght animation ever released in Japan.
