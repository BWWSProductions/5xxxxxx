The Snow Queen (Danish: Sneedronningen) is a fairy tale by author Hans Christian Andersen (1805 - 1875). The tale was 

first published in 1845, and centers on the struggle between good and evil as experienced by a little boy and girl, Kai 

and Gerda.

The story is one of Andersen's longest and is considered by scholars, critics, and readers alike as one of his best. It is 

regularly included in selected tales and collections of his work as well as being frequently reprinted in illustrated 

storybook editions for children. The tale has been adapted in various media including animated film and television 

drama.
