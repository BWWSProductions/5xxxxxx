A pair of masses by Gounod and Rossini, plus Verdi's Te Deum.

The Gounod is wonderfully over-the-top.  There is a lot of saccharine sweetness to it, almost like much modern film music.  Its none the worse for it, and was apparently hugely popular in his time.

Rossini's mass is the opposite, being camp rather than grand, with the soloists and choir being accompanied by a harmonium and a pair of pianos. Whereas the Gounod is primarily singing by a chorus with passages for soloist, the Rossini reverses this, and is therefore far more "operatic" in style.

I'm not particularly familiar with, or keen on, the Verdi on the second CD, but each to their own :)

The two logged defects on CD2 are minor (99.9% accurate) and are inaudible.
