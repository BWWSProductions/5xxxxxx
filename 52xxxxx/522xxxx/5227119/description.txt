Taking Woodstock is a 2009 American comedy-drama film about the Woodstock Festival of 1969 directed by Ang Lee. The screenplay by James Schamus is based on the memoir Taking Woodstock: A True Story of a Riot, a Concert, and a Life by Elliot Tiber and Tom Monte.[1] The film premiered at the 2009 Cannes Film Festival,[2] and opened in New York and Los Angeles on August 26, 2009 before its major theatrical release two days later.
