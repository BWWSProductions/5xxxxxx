    >> SYNOPSiS <<

    This 4 part documentary tells the tale of the
    rise of the NSDAP - the Nationalist Socialist
    Workers Party of Germany - and of the importance
    that occult doctrines played in it's foundation,
    and of the course it was to set.

    Since the early days of the Thule Society (the 
    Munich lodge of the nationalistic Germanen Order),
    that was later to become the NSDAP, it's program
    and beliefs was heavily centered around a
    nationalistic and occult agenda.

    PART 1 - The Enigma of the Swastika
    -----------------------------------

    This part tells the story of the Swastika -
    an ancient symbol that has it's historic roots
    in the religous belief systems in India and
    Tibet. Beginning during the Great War, the
    Swastika found it's way to the battlefields of
    Europe, and was considered to be a symbol of
    fortune, luck and good health - and it grew
    popular not only in Germany, but in the whole
    of the western world.

    During the 1920:s, various nationalistic move-
    ments in Germany started to use the Swastika
    as their head symbol. One such movement was
    the infamous Thule Society - a group which 
    later was to be transformed to the Nationalist
    Socialist Workers Party of Germany. For this
    movement, the Swastika and it's mystical
    meaning became a central focus point.

    PART 2 - The SS: Blood and Soil
    -------------------------------

    As the NSDAP grew larger, and Adolf Hitlers
    popularity increased, he felt forced to 
    create a group of loyal body guards that
    was to protect him during public rallys and
    speeches. This group was to become the SS - 
    the Schutzstaffel.

    Imediately from the start of it's formation,
    the SS became known amongst party members as
    "the hardcore" and "the elite". The SS rapidly
    developed to become a fanatic religio-military
    group, devoted to fully adopt the mystical  
    doctrines of the Nationalist Socialist movement, 
    and to stand by their F├â┬╝hrer at all costs - 
    without questioning, and until the bitter end.

    PART 3 - Himmler: the Mystic
    ----------------------------

    The enigmatic figure Heinrich Himmler, leader
    and head chief of the SS, was from an early
    age obsessed with astrology, spirituality and
    the occult.

    As an early member of the NSDAP, and participant
    in the "Beer Hall" rally in 1923 - an organized
    raid where nationalistic movements tried to
    overthrow the german government - he rapidly
    became close friend with Hitler. Himmlers 
    mystical beliefs played a vital role in the
    shaping of the SS.

    PART 4 - Adolf Hitler
    ---------------------

    The final part of the documentary tells the story
    of Adolf Hitler, a desillusioned austrian World
    War 1-veteran, that was sent out by the military to
    infiltrate the Thule Society - a movement that of
    which he later became the leader and front figure.

    Hitler strongly believed in the re-union of all
    germanic regions of Europe based on the common
    cultural and historic heritage, and that he was
    the man set out to lead this task. He was also a firm
    believer in the mystical and mythical doctrines
    layed out by occultists such as Helena Blavatsky and
    Guido von List - doctrines that grew more and more 
    popular amongst the germans during the 1920:s.
